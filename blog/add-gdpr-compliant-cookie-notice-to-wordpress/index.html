How to Easily Add GDPR-Compliant Cookie Notice to WordPress Websites

Published on March 27, 2025

Today, having a digital environment is essential, businesses need to have a website in the Internet world. And when you own a website, you must prioritize GDPR compliance and other data privacy laws & regulations. That is where comes the Cookie Notice.

Let’s learn about cookie notices and how you can effortlessly add a GDPR-compliant cookie notice to WordPress websites. We will provide you with step-by-step instructions on how to configure it swiftly and effectively.

Why Do You Need a GDPR-Compliant Cookie Notice In WordPress?

A cookie notice informs site visitors about your website’s use of cookies to collect data. Cookies, the small pieces of data such as your preferences, login details, shopping cart contents, etc, are stored by the website on your device when you visit them. They help websites remember information about your visit to make your next browsing experience more personalized and effective.

Under GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation), a website owner must obtain user consent before storing cookies, ensuring transparency and compliance with data protection laws. A cookie notice secures the consent of the visitors before collecting any data from them.

There are, of course, some benefits of adding cookie notices to your website. Providing enough information to your site visitors about why collecting their data is necessary. Otherwise, it can lead to penalties and loss of user trust. Let us see the benefits of adding a cookie notice.

Ensures Legal Compliance

By displaying a cookie notice, you help your website stay compliant with GDPR, CCPA (California Consumer Privacy Act), and other data privacy laws. This minimizes the risk of potential fines and legal consequences due to non-compliance.

Builds Trust And Credibility

Transparency about data collection reassures visitors that their privacy is respected. This builds trust and strengthens your brand’s credibility, encouraging users to stay engaged with your website.

Enhances User Control & Experience

A well-designed cookie notice allows users to choose whether to accept or reject cookies, giving them greater control over their personal data. This aligns with ethical data practices and enhances the overall user experience.

Reduces Bounce Rate & Increases Engagement

Visitors are more likely to stay on a website when they feel confident about how their data is being handled. A clear cookie notice prevents immediate exits, helping reduce bounce rates and improve site engagement.

Supports Transparency & Data Ethics

In an era where data privacy is a growing concern, being upfront about cookie usage demonstrates your commitment to ethical data practices. This can differentiate your website from competitors that may not prioritize user privacy.

How to Display Cookie Notices to Your Website

Now that you know its importance, you might be wondering how to add cookie notices to your website. Well, you can add GDPR-compliant cookie notices to your website using various methods mentioned below:

  • Manually code a cookie notice, which requires technical coding knowledge. 
  • Use Google Tag Manager (GTM) for consent management by creating a cookie consent banner.
  • Implement a cookie policy page or update the privacy policy & terms incorporating cookie policies. 
  • Enable Google Consent Mode for Google Analytics or Ads to adjust tracking based on user preferences.
  • Apply using a cookie consent plugin if you are using WordPress or another CMS. 

You can choose your preferred method from the above-mentioned process. To us, using a plugin is the easiest and quickest way, as there is no need for complex technical knowledge to add a GDPR-compliant cookie notice to your site.

Step 1: Install And Activate NotificationX

First, from the WordPress repository, you need to install the NotificationX plugin and activate it on your site. For that, simply go to the WordPress dashboard and click on the ‘Add New Plugin’ from the ‘Plugins’ tab. 

Then, type NotificationX on the Search Plugin field and you will get the plugin. Click on the ‘Install Now’ button and then click on the ‘Activate’ button.

Step 2: Add a New Notification And Choose A Design

From the WordPress dashboard, click on NotificationX. Then, from the Sources tab, click on the ‘Add New’ button. Give a title to your notice & select ‘Cookie Notice’ as the Notification Type. By default, ‘GDPR’ will be selected as the Source – all you need to do is click on the ‘Next’ button to proceed.

Now, select a theme for your WordPress Cookie Notice from different design options. You can also choose the position where the cookie notice will appear on the screen from the dropdown options. To access advanced design options, toggle the ‘Advanced Design’ button. Once you are satisfied with the design, click ‘Next‘.

Step 3: Add & Customize Cookie Notice Contents 

In the ‘Content’ tab, provide the necessary information for your cookie notice. This includes adding a ‘Title’, a ‘Message,’ and customizing the ‘Button Texts.’ You can also toggle the button to enable the cookie policy link and add the relevant link to your cookie policy for the website. 

In the ‘Preference Center’ Section, add a ‘Title’ and an ‘Overview’ detailing your cookie preferences. In the ‘Cookie List’ section, toggle the button to display the cookie list in the notice. You can also add a label for the cookie list and modify the button text. Once finished, click on ‘Next’.

Step 4: Configure Your Cookie Settings & Publish

In the ‘Manager’ tab, toggle the buttons to enable or disable the ‘Force Reload’ and ‘Cookie Removal’ options. You can also set a Consent Expiry Date to determine when users will need to re-consent to your cookie policy. 

In the ‘Cookie List’ section, customize the different categories of cookies by clicking the ‘Edit’ icon to modify the category name and description. Then, click on the Next button. Here, you can adjust your visibility and animation settings. Once you have configured your cookie notice, click the ‘Publish’ button to make it live and visible to your website visitors.
