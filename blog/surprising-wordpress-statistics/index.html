17+ Surprising WordPress Statistics You Should Not Miss [2024]

Published on December 23, 2023

WordPress has solidified its position as a powerhouse in the world of website creation and content management. It has emerged as the go-to content management system for individuals and businesses alike. Its remarkable growth makes it one of the most popular open-source CMS in the world.

In this article, we will delve into a comprehensive exploration of 17+ surprising WordPress statistics, especially focusing on its market dominance, user base, plugins, themes, and more. Let us dive in. 

WordPress Usage: Why Is It Insanely Popular?

WordPress continues to stand out as a versatile and powerful content management system. It has many eye-opening interesting WordPress statistics that showcase its widespread adoption. Before diving into the WordPress statistics, let us explore the reasons behind its success. WordPress is one of the best CMS and a first choice for website development. It has garnered immense popularity for several compelling reasons: 

⭐ WordPress is an open-source software. This feature empowers users with the freedom to customize and modify their websites according to their unique needs, fostering a vibrant community of developers and contributors. 

⭐ The platform offers a wide variety of themes and plugins, allowing users to enhance functionality and design effortlessly. This versatility contributes to its widespread adoption by businesses, bloggers, and developers alike. 

⭐ WordPress is renowned for its user-friendly interface, facilitating the creation of multipurpose websites without requiring extensive technical expertise. 

⭐ Its robust SEO capabilities make it an ideal choice for those seeking visibility and ranking in search engine results, further solidifying its popularity in the digital landscape.

17+ Surprising WordPress Statistics For 2024 You Must Know

WordPress has become the most popular content management system, revolutionizing the way businesses and individuals create and manage websites. With its open-source nature and user-friendly interface, WordPress has captured the hearts of millions around the globe. Let us delve into 17+ surprising WordPress statistics that highlight the platform’s dominance and impact in today’s world.

1. Ranking In Search Engine With High Volume

One of the amazing WordPress facts is that a search for “WordPress” on Google returns a jaw-dropping 1,900,000,000 results in 0.28 seconds, highlighting the platform’s immense popularity and relevance. This number reflects the widespread interest, discussions, and inquiries surrounding WordPress, signifying its status as a dominant force in the digital space.

2. Market Dominance: Overall Market Share

WordPress’s staggering 62.9% share in the CMS market. It is intriguing because it underscores its immense influence on the internet landscape, showcasing a widespread preference for its user-friendly content management system.

3. Rapid Adoption Of Updates: WordPress Versions

With 77.4% of WordPress sites running on version 6.X, the community’s quick adoption of the latest updates is noteworthy. It reflects the active engagement of developers and the importance placed on maintaining compatibility and security across the WordPress ecosystem.

4. Global Impact Across Industries: Market Trends

The continuous influence of WordPress in shaping various industries, as indicated by tailored statistics reflecting its impact, showcases its adaptability and relevance across diverse sectors, further solidifying its position as a versatile content management solution. According to predictions, the usage of WordPress in the tech industry could rise by up to 15% in 2024.

5. Developer Engagement: Compatibility & Support 

One of the surprising WordPress statistics is that WordPress version  5.X still active on 17.2% of WordPress sites. It highlights that developers are mindful of compatibility and support for older versions. This emphasizes the ecosystem’s commitment to ensuring a smooth experience for users across various iterations of the platform.

5. Dominance In Top 10 Million Websites

The fact that WordPress drives 43.1% of the top 10 million websites globally demonstrates its prowess in handling high-traffic sites. This has established its reputation not just among small websites but also as a reliable choice for major online platforms.

6. Live Sites Milestone: Reliability and Performance

The milestone of 34,286,296 live sites actively using WordPress in 2023 proves its reliability and consistent performance. This vast number of active sites reinforces WordPress’s status as a reliable choice for website creators across different domains. The platform continues to shape the online landscape with its versatility and user-friendly features.

7. WordPress Plugin Directory

The official WordPress plugin directory boasts 59,483 plugins, providing users with a vast array of options to enhance their websites. These plugins have been downloaded over 17 billion times, reflecting their popularity and impact in the WordPress ecosystem.

8. WordPress Theme Repository

The official WordPress theme repository houses thousands of free themes [currently 11,675, till 2 January 2024], allowing users to customize the appearance and design of their websites. Additionally, there are over 20,000 WordPress themes available, including premium options, catering to users’ diverse needs and preferences.

9. SEO Support

WordPress boasts a remarkable support rate of 80% to 90% for SEO mechanics. It makes WordPress an ideal platform for users aiming to optimize their websites for search engines. Its SEO-friendly features and plugins contribute to its reputation as a powerful tool for digital marketing.

10. WooCommerce Dominance

WooCommerce, a subcategory of WordPress is the most widely used eCommerce platform. According to a WooCommerce report, 3.9M online stores are built with WooCommerce which is 43% of the total website built on WordPress. Also, WordPress is powering up 23% of the top 1 million eCommerce sites. Its seamless integration with WordPress allows users to create robust and scalable e-commerce solutions.

11. WordPress Security

WordPress is generally considered secure; however, it is not immune to data breaches and vulnerabilities. Sucuri, a security plugin, reported that 90% of their cleanup requests in 2018 were related to WordPress websites.

12. Multilingual Support

WordPress supports up to 110+ different languages, making it accessible to users from various regions and cultures. Its commitment to inclusivity has contributed to its global appeal.

13. Content Publishing Popularity

Users publish approximately 5 Million blog posts everyday on WordPress, highlighting the platform’s popularity as a content publishing tool. Its user-friendly editor and seamless publishing process have made it a favored choice for bloggers and content creators.

14. Daily Website Development

Developers use WordPress to develop more than 500 websites every day, highlighting the platform’s versatility and appeal to web development professionals. Its extensive plugin directory and customizable themes make it an ideal choice for developers. 

15. Average Theme Price

The average price of a WordPress theme is around $59, providing users with affordable options to enhance the visual appeal of their websites. Theme subscription memberships offer access to a variety of themes for a yearly cost, allowing users to explore different designs and styles.

16. WooCommerce In eCommerce

Among 1 million eCommerce websites, 22% are based on WooCommerce, further strengthening its position as a leading eCommerce solution. Its user-friendly interface and extensive features make it a favored choice among online retailers.

17. WordPress Searches

WordPress generates over 37 million searches per month worldwide, showcasing the platform’s relevance and impact in the online world. Users turn to WordPress for guidance, support, and solutions to their website-related queries. 

18. Installed Plugins On WordPress

Over 1 million websites have all of the top 30 WordPress plugins installed, showcasing the widespread adoption and trust in these essential tools. From SEO optimization to security measures, these plugins contribute to the success of WordPress websites.
